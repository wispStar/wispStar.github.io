# "Little Scientists" went to the "Science T Conference" for the third time

What is the youth of Huda people like? At Hunan University, there are as many brilliant youthful looks as there are young students. "Youth School" character column, let's learn about the story of Ni Jiazhou, a 2021 undergraduate student in the School of Information Science and Engineering.

Recently, the first major event of the 5th World Laureates Forum **"Science T Conference" was held in Shanghai**. Ni Jiazhou, a 2021 undergraduate student majoring in computer science and technology in the School of Information Science and Engineering of our school, "successfully chased stars" at the scene! He presented "A Piece of Stromatolite", a collection of the results of his and his classmates' innovative practices in high school, to Professor Wang Pingxian, a famous marine geologist and academician of the Chinese Academy of Sciences, and received encouragement from his "idol": "Continue to maintain a passion for science. Be passionate and strive to become a great scientist from a young scientist.”

## "Little Scientist" went to the "Science T Conference" for the third time

In the second year of high school, he was selected as an outstanding student of the "Talent Program" of the China Association for Science and Technology, and became one of only ten in the country to be selected as the fourth WLF "Little Scientist". Ni Jiazhou has been invited to participate in the World Laureates Forum three times in a row and has been reported by CCTV News twice. **"A Piece of Stromatolite"** records the research-based learning and innovative practical activities carried out by Ni Jiazhou and his classmates over the past few years. Why are they called stromatolites? Ni Jiazhou said: Stromatolites are the oldest organisms on the earth. They grow layer by layer over time, just like scientific research develops on the basis of predecessors. Moreover, **we should not be blind followers of certain conclusions, but should strive to be leaders in innovative practice**.

Ni Jiazhou’s relationship with scientific research can be traced back to the Changzhou “We Love Science” activity he participated in in fifth grade. "Exploring new knowledge through experiments is a particularly interesting thing." Since then, he has fallen in love with the laboratory. "It is common to spend several hours in the laboratory." Digital experiments explore the antacid ability of antacids , Quantitative research on the preparation of household chlorine-containing disinfectants by electrolyzing salt water, SLAM technology application: low-cost 3D video special effects editing technology, social logistics: an inclusive and sustainable logistics development plan... From high school to university, Ni Jiazhou has participated in a variety of topics Despite diversity, **the only thing that remains unchanged is the passion and persistence for scientific research**.

Recently, Ni Jiazhou is working on a scientific research project related to sustainable development. The 12 members of the research team come from more than 10 majors in 7 universities. **He said that he hopes to make some real things with his partners and make some positive contributions to society**.

## "Slash Youth" outside of scientific research

Not only is he actively exploring computers and sustainable development, but he also loves music, painting, and photography. Ni Jiazhou said bluntly: "I want to try more possibilities in life." The violin has been his hobby since childhood; he also uses lenses to record what he sees and hears. Currently, Ni Jiazhou is already a "Visual China Contract Photographer"; he also enjoys sketching, Chinese painting and even intangible cultural heritage "pyrography". On the night of November 8 this year, he and his friends climbed up to Yuelu Mountain and recorded the astronomical spectacle of the total lunar eclipse with their lenses. The photography was published in **Beijing Youth Daily**.

He is also a student lecturer in the quality development **"Pyrography" and "Photography" classes** of the Student Affairs Department. He combines theory and practice in the classroom and invites students to experience the charm of art together. **He also actively participated in science popularization lectures and symphony orchestras... This reflects the infinite possibilities of college life. “Don’t be a blind follower of conclusions, and strive to be a leader in innovative practice.”** Ni Jiazhou is full of enthusiasm and expectations for the future, “I hope to create more possibilities with our partners.”
