# Summer Solstice Encounters "Solar Eclipse"

Unwilling to enjoy an annular solar eclipse in a circle of friends, a high school student waited for more than an hour on a cloudy day to capture a partial solar eclipse in a Changzhou neighborhood. Others traveled to Xiamen just to see this astronomical spectacle in its entirety.

## Summer solstice meets an annular solar eclipse

The summer solstice encountered an annular solar eclipse, the weather in Changzhou cloudy and rainy, we are mostly in the circle of friends "cloud" to appreciate this rare celestial wonders. **Unbelievable, in Changzhou Xintiandi Garden neighborhood, there are people with a camera captured a clear picture of the partial eclipse**, the shooter **Ni Jiazhou** is Changzhou Senior High School of Jiangsu Province sophomore (11) class students. Ni was attracted by the vastness of the universe in his childhood, read a lot of astronomy books, and joined the school astronomy club after he went to high school.

This time, he learned that he could observe a partial solar eclipse in Changzhou on June 21, and used simulation software to obtain the time point and orientation of the partial solar eclipse in Tianning District, from the first loss to the eclipse and then to the recirculation. At about 2:00 pm yesterday, he and his father, who live in Xintiandi Garden, came to the square of the neighborhood and set up their cameras to keep watch. Around 3:10 p.m., the sun finally briefly opened the "veil". He immediately used the camera to record the wonderful moment, more than 20 minutes to shoot more than 100 photos, recorded the partial eclipse after the first loss to the sun was covered 1/3 of the moment, "light clouds swept through the formation of shades of light and shadow, there is a kind of 'colorful clouds chasing the moon, the moon by the sun,' the poetic feeling. "

Such a clear picture from the Sony micro-single, Ni introduced, shooting focal length of 210 mm, aperture 6.3, shutter speed 1/50, "chose the right camera position and parameters, simple equipment can also shoot a blockbuster."

**"Yesterday on Weibo there was a saying: the annular solar eclipse meets Father's Day, like a father who always shades himself from the scorching sun."** Little Ni said, this is the most touching words he has ever heard, and observing the partial solar eclipse with his dad yesterday was the best way to celebrate the holiday.

## Special trip to Xiamen seaside to witness the celestial spectacle first hand

And as astronomy fans totally, more than a dozen people in Changzhou have traveled to Xiamen to witness this celestial spectacle in order to catch a shot of the eclipse, and have also photographed the process of its formation.

Lu Yao, 50 years old, is one of the most experienced astronomy fans in Changzhou, who introduced that this solar eclipse is the only time in this century that an annular solar eclipse will be staged on the summer solstice in China, which is very special and rare. Changzhou is in the rainy season, so he began to pay close attention to the weather across the country a week in advance, until June 19th before finally decided to chase the shooting place in Xiamen seaside, and on the 20th arrived at the destination. "Xiamen is sunny and stable, with thin clouds, suitable for observation and shooting."

The last two times Lu Yao photographed the eclipse were in 2010 and 2012, and he was very excited to photograph the eclipse again after eight years. From about 2:00 p.m. to end at about 5:30 p.m., this trip to Xiamen, he took a total of hundreds of photos of the solar eclipse.

According to reports, Lu Yao fell in love with astronomy when Halley's Comet returned in 1986. In order to meet once every 76 years, Lu Yao, who was still in high school at the time, prepared for a year and "chewed" a batch of astronomy books. "At that time, the equipment was very simple. I put together toilet paper tubes, wax paper tubes, mounted convex lenses and concave lenses, made simple monocular astronomical telescope. Now, in addition to professional astronomical telescopes, we also use equatorial instruments to track the trajectory of eclipses." Lu Yao said that witnessing and recording an astronomical spectacle is the happiest thing for astronomy fans.
